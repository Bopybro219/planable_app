Cookies

Session and sign-in basics

Planira keeps this simple. Necessary cookies support sign-in and security, while analytics and advertising cookies stay off unless you explicitly allow them.

What Planira uses cookies for

Login and session state Planira currently uses one Flask session cookie to keep you signed in and connect requests to the right account.
Security for signed-in actions Planira also uses session-backed security tokens to help protect forms and account actions.
Turnstile anti-abuse checks Protected forms may load Cloudflare Turnstile to reduce spam and automated abuse. This is separate from advertising or analytics tracking.
Email preferences Newsletter opt-in and unsubscribe preferences are stored on the server, but Planira does not use cookies to track newsletter engagement.
Consent preferences Planira stores your cookie choices in a small consent cookie so the site can remember whether optional analytics or advertising is allowed on future visits.

What Planira does not use cookies for

No advertising before consent Planira does not load Google AdSense or advertising cookies unless you opt in to the marketing category.
Analytics only after consent If Google Analytics 4 is enabled in a live environment, it only loads after you opt in. Planira uses it for high-level product events and does not send personal data such as names, email addresses, support messages, or exact addresses.
Advertising may use Google cookies after consent If AdSense is enabled and you opt in to marketing cookies, Google may use cookies or similar technologies for personalised or non-personalised ads depending on your settings, location, and Google’s own systems.

Keeping this accurate

If Planira changes its analytics setup, advertising placements, or other cookie-based features later, this page should be updated before those changes are treated as normal. Today, analytics stays off until consent is given, advertising only loads after marketing consent on supported public pages, and transactional emails, support replies, and opt-in newsletters do not rely on ad-tracking cookies.
